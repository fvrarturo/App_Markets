===============================
EQUITIES
===============================

What the desk does:
- Trades stocks, ETFs, and equity derivatives to provide liquidity and market access.
- Focuses on client execution, risk management, and pricing volatility (options).
- In London: heavy focus on European equities, structured equity, and financing (prime brokerage).

How to view indicators:
- Rising indices (EuroStoxx, FTSE, DAX) → risk-on tone, equity traders long-biased.
- Falling implied vol (VIX/VStoxx) → quieter markets, more structured-yield issuance.
- Widening vol → hedging demand, index options pricing more expensive.
- Sector performance shifts (banks vs tech) → rotation themes, affect flow and hedging.

Questions to ask:
- "Which sectors are driving most of your flow recently?"
- "How does lower volatility affect your book or client activity?"
- "Do you see more demand for yield structures or protection lately?"
- "How do you hedge large client orders when liquidity dries up?"

News keywords:
equities, EuroStoxx, FTSE 100, VIX, VStoxx, earnings, buybacks, volatility, sector rotation, valuation, structured notes, autocallables, hedge funds.


===============================
RATES
===============================

What the desk does:
- Trades government bonds, swaps, and rate derivatives.
- Manages interest rate exposure, curve shape (2s10s, 5s30s), and policy expectations.
- Provides hedging to corporates, sovereigns, and funds.

How to view indicators:
- Rising yields → tightening policy or higher inflation expectations.
- Falling yields → risk-off or recession fears.
- Curve steepening → easing expectations or fiscal supply.
- Curve flattening → tighter policy, growth worries.
- Volatility index (MOVE) → measures rate uncertainty; high MOVE = more trading opportunity.

Questions to ask:
- "How are you positioned on the curve—expecting more steepening or flattening?"
- "What’s driving the long end: fiscal issuance or term premium?"
- "How sensitive is your book to ECB/BOE policy expectations?"

News keywords:
yield curve, ECB, BOE, Fed, Bund, Gilt, Treasury, inflation swaps, 2s10s, 5s30s, MOVE index, QT, fiscal deficit, sovereign issuance.


===============================
FX
===============================

What the desk does:
- Trades currency pairs (spot, forwards, swaps, options).
- Provides liquidity, hedging, and speculative access to FX markets.
- London is the global hub for G10 and EMEA currencies.

How to view indicators:
- DXY up → USD strength; often risk-off.
- Carry differentials → drive EM and high-yield currency flows.
- FX vol rising → option traders busy, more hedging flows.
- Basis swaps widening → USD funding tightness.

Questions to ask:
- "Where do you see most client flow—hedging or speculation?"
- "How have carry trades behaved with recent rate differentials?"
- "What’s been driving EUR/USD skew—more demand for calls or puts?"

News keywords:
forex, FX, EUR/USD, GBP/USD, DXY, carry trade, vol surface, risk reversal, EM FX, cross-currency basis, BOE, ECB, Fed, intervention.


===============================
COMMODITIES
===============================

What the desk does:
- Trades energy (oil, gas, power), metals, and soft commodities.
- Provides hedging and structured solutions to corporates and investors.
- London desks often focus on energy, emissions, and metals markets.

How to view indicators:
- Rising crude prices → energy traders active; inflation pressure → affects rates & equities.
- Backwardation vs contango → reveals physical tightness or surplus.
- Volatility spikes in oil/nat gas → more client hedging, bigger P&L swings.
- Metals prices (copper, gold) → global demand proxies and risk sentiment.

Questions to ask:
- "What drives most of your client flow—hedging or directional views?"
- "How are you managing volatility in energy markets lately?"
- "Are clients still using structured products around oil prices?"

News keywords:
oil, Brent, WTI, natural gas, OPEC, metals, copper, gold, LNG, energy volatility, emissions, carbon trading.


===============================
CREDIT
===============================

What the desk does:
- Trades corporate bonds, credit default swaps (CDS), and loan products.
- Provides liquidity and hedging for investors exposed to credit risk.
- London desks: focus on European investment-grade (IG) and high-yield (HY) credit.

How to view indicators:
- Tightening spreads → improving sentiment, risk-on.
- Widening spreads → risk aversion, rising default risk.
- iTraxx Main and Xover → benchmark indices for IG and HY credit spreads.
- New issuance spikes → positive sign for market confidence.

Questions to ask:
- "Are you seeing more flow in IG or HY lately?"
- "How are clients positioned—buying protection or selling?"
- "Do you think spreads reflect fundamentals or just liquidity flows?"

News keywords:
credit spreads, CDS, iTraxx, default rates, HY, IG, issuance, leverage loans, rating downgrade, refinancing.


===============================
SPG (Securitized Products Group)
===============================

What the desk does:
- Trades and structures securities backed by loans (RMBS, CMBS, ABS, CLOs).
- Connects lenders/investors via securitization, managing yield and risk tranching.
- London focus: European RMBS, ABS, and CLOs.

How to view indicators:
- Widening RMBS/ABS spreads → stress in housing or consumer credit.
- New issuance levels → gauge investor appetite and funding conditions.
- Default and prepayment data → key to valuation and performance.
- CLO tranche spreads → signal credit-cycle sentiment.

Questions to ask:
- "How has issuance evolved given higher rates?"
- "Are investors demanding more protection in mezz tranches?"
- "Which asset classes (RMBS, autos, CLOs) are most active right now?"

News keywords:
RMBS, CMBS, ABS, CLO, securitization, prepayment, default, consumer credit, housing market, structured credit, tranche spreads.


===============================
STRUCTURED PRODUCTS
===============================

What the desk does:
- Designs and prices customized derivatives/notes combining asset classes.
- Balances client objectives (yield, protection, leverage) with hedge feasibility.
- London focus: equity-linked, credit-linked, and cross-asset payoffs.

How to view indicators:
- Low volatility → cheaper options → more yield-enhancement demand.
- High volatility → more hedging/protection products.
- Rate levels → affect coupon potential and product attractiveness.
- Correlation shifts → impact multi-asset payoff structures.

Questions to ask:
- "What kinds of payoffs are clients demanding right now—yield or protection?"
- "How do you hedge correlation risk across multi-asset structures?"
- "Have you seen more cross-asset or ESG-linked demand recently?"

News keywords:
structured notes, autocallables, reverse convertibles, hybrid structures, volatility, correlation, client demand, product issuance, ESG, retail structured products.
